In a Few Words

My name is Alex Eastman. I'm a computer engineer in-training and teaching assistant for introductory computer science at the University at Buffalo. I'm a focused, driven, and hard-working individual with a passion for technology. Although I'll be trained in both electrical engineering and computer science as part of my major, I'd like to focus on expanding my abilities on the latter. More About Me

Student

I enjoy learning. I'd go as far as to say that learning is my primary goal in life, and while I've chosen my career to be in computers, they're not necessarily the bounds of my interest. I listen to podcasts, I read the New York Times, and I keep an as-of-yet small collection of For Dummies books. In conversation, I've been known to ask a lot of questions. Some might call it being nosy, but in truth it's because I'm genuinely curious about the other person. Everyone I meet has taken a different path to be where they are, and it fascinates me to learn their story and background.


Teaching Assistant

I began as a teaching assistant in the fall of my sophomore year, 2019. Along with learning, I enjoy teaching as well. I'm very passionate about my area of study, and so it makes me happy when I can share that with other people. I want my students to look up to me, as I did with my teaching assistants, and so I strive to set a good example for them and deliver on my obligation to help them learn. The course that I'm an assistant for is introductory computer science, where students learn the basics of syntax, languages, control flow, and file I/O. I lead labs for two hours at a time where students can ask me questions on the activity, whether it be an error message or the best way to approach a problem. Often times, my labs are the best periods of my day.
